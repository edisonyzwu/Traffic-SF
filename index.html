San Francisco Traffic Deaths Remain High 10 Years After "Vison Zero"

By Edison Wu | Oct.3, 2024

In 2014, San Francisco committed to "Vision Zero," a bold initiative aimed at eliminating traffic deaths within a decade. City officials promised to invest millions of dollars to reduce speeds and redesign intersections to prioritize pedestrian safety.

However, a decade later, the results fall short. Data shows that more than twenty-five people lost their lives in traffic crashes in San Francisco in 2024, nearly matching the number of deaths recorded in 2014.
